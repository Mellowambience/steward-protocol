# FAQ

**Is The Steward Project a company?**
No. It is an open protocol and a body of writing. Anyone may implement it.

**Is it a model or an app?**
Neither. It is words: a Constitution, a Language, a Standard, and a Manifesto.
Software implements it; the protocol belongs to everyone.

**What is AetherTwin, then?**
The reference implementation — one beautiful example of a Steward Compatible
system. Not the project itself.

**Do I have to use your model / your OS / your tools?**
No. The protocol is model-agnostic and OS-agnostic. A coding tutor on Linux,
an educational companion on a phone, a lab collaborator in the cloud — all can
be Steward Compatible.

**What does "Steward Compatible" certify?**
That a system meets the six requirements in `STANDARD.md`: Reflection Loop,
Assumption Tracking, Human Override, Evidence Logging, Growth Memory, and
Transparent Uncertainty. It certifies *behavior*, not intelligence.

**Can the badge be revoked?**
Yes. Compatibility is a practice. The community can challenge and revoke it if a
system is shown to violate the Protocol.

**How do I start?**
Read `CONSTITUTION.md`, adopt the `LANGUAGE.md` in your own work, self-assess
against `STANDARD.md` (see `examples/`), and build. The soil is free.
