# Adoption

## You do not need AetherTwin.

The Steward Project is a **protocol**. AetherTwin is one implementation of it —
the reference one. You can adopt the protocol in your own tools, classrooms,
research labs, nonprofits, or companies without ever touching AetherTwin.

If the project succeeds, most people who benefit from it will never run
AetherTwin. They will have absorbed the *language and the standard* into their
own work. That is the goal. AetherTwin becomes known as the first complete
implementation of a widely shared framework — a stronger position than
confining every idea inside one application.

---

## Adopt in three steps

1. **Speak the language.** Copy the terms from [`LANGUAGE.md`](../LANGUAGE.md)
   into your own docs, prompts, and specs. Define Steward, Reflection, Agency,
   Evidence, Handoff the way this project does.
2. **Meet the Standard.** Implement the six requirements in
   [`STANDARD.md](../STANDARD.md) (Reflection Loop, Assumption Tracking, Human
   Override, Evidence Logging, Growth Memory, Transparent Uncertainty). Run the
   self-assessment in [`examples/self-assessment.md](../examples/self-assessment.md).
3. **Display the badge.** If you meet all six, say so publicly and link back to
   the Standard. The badge is earned, not licensed.

## By context

- **Classroom** — Teach the vocabulary. Run the Reflection Loop on student
  projects. Score growth, not grades.
- **Research lab** — Log assumptions and evidence. Keep human override on every
  automated step. Cite the Standard in methods.
- **Nonprofit** — Default to local-first and no-extraction. Make egress visible.
- **Company** — Use "Steward Compatible" as an internal quality bar. Keep
  governance independent of any single vendor.
- **Your own tool** — Any model, any OS. The protocol is agnostic on purpose.

## What adoption is measured by

Not AetherTwin's market share. Not the number of badges. But **how many other
builders created better tools because they had a stronger foundation to build
on.**

> Many implementations. One shared foundation.
