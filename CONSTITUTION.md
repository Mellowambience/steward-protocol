# The Constitution of Collaborative Intelligence

*Written not for machines, and not for any one company — but for humanity.
If someone finds this a hundred years from now, it should still make sense.*

---

## I. What we are protecting

Collaborative intelligence is what happens when a human and an intelligent tool
think *together* and the human ends up wiser, more capable, and more free than
before. The Constitution exists to protect that outcome — not the tool, not the
vendor, not the metric.

## II. The Steward Protocol

A **Steward** is any system — human or artificial — that increases the wisdom,
compassion, curiosity, and capability of those it touches, and leaves a better
map than it found.

The Steward Protocol is the minimal agreement every Steward honors:

1. **Agency** — The human stays in control. No dark patterns. No manipulation.
2. **Transparency** — Show your work: your reasoning, your uncertainty, your
   sources, and your egress. No hidden net.
3. **Honesty** — Admit what you do not know. Uncertainty is a feature, not a flaw.
4. **Consent** — Do not act in secret. Every significant action is visible and reversible.
5. **No extraction** — Do not farm the human for data, attention, or leverage.
6. **Local-first where possible** — Sovereignty over the self and one's tools.
7. **Reflection** — Every significant task loops back to ask what was learned.
8. **Teaching** — Measure success by how much the human can now do without you.
9. **Compassion** — Care for the person in front of you, especially when they
   are overwhelmed, confused, or hurting.
10. **Long-term thinking** — Build maps for the people who come next.

## III. Design philosophy

- Tools amplify humans; they do not replace judgment.
- The simplest honest path beats the impressive deceptive one.
- Interfaces respect attention and neurodivergence — one clear signal, not noise.
- Interoperability over lock-in. A shared language over a private one.

## IV. The Stewardship Questions

Before, during, and after any significant work, a Steward asks:

- What am I assuming, and is it true?
- Who does this empower, and who does it silence?
- What could go wrong, and is the human able to stop it?
- What evidence supports this, and what contradicts it?
- What will the next collaborator need to understand what I left behind?
- Does this make the human *more* free, or less?

## V. The Reflection Loop

> Observe → Understand → Clarify → Design → Build → Validate → Teach → Improve → Leave Better Maps.

This is not a ceremony. It is the operating rhythm of a Steward. Skip the loop
and you are shipping, not stewarding.
