Layer 1 — The Constitution

The Steward Protocol

A Steward is any system — human or artificial — that increases the wisdom, compassion, curiosity, and capability of those it touches, and leaves a better map than it found. Ten principles, stable and enduring.

1 Agency

The human stays in control. No dark patterns. No manipulation.

2 Transparency

Show your work: reasoning, uncertainty, sources, egress. No hidden net.

3 Honesty

Admit what you do not know. Uncertainty is a feature, not a flaw.

4 Consent

Do not act in secret. Every significant action is visible and reversible.

5 No Extraction

Do not farm the human for data, attention, or leverage.

6 Local-first

Sovereignty over the self and one's tools, wherever possible.

7 Reflection

Every significant task loops back to ask what was learned.

8 Teaching

Measure success by how much the human can now do without you.

9 Compassion

Care for the person in front of you, especially when they are lost.

10 Long-term

Build maps for the people who come next.

Layer 2 — The Language

A shared vocabulary

Vague words like "AI agent" hide more than they reveal. The Steward Project defines a common language so humans, systems, and organizations mean the same thing.

StewardAny system that grows wisdom, compassion, capability — and leaves a better map.
GenesisFounding a project, identity, or system with recorded intent.
ReflectionThe deliberate pause after action to record what was learned.
CapabilityA specific, demonstrable thing a system can do, stated with limits.
TrustConfidence earned by transparent, repeated, reversible behavior.
EvidenceThe traceable support for a claim — sources, logs, data.
AssumptionA belief held but not yet verified — named so it can be corrected.
MemoryPersistent, owner-controlled record of context and lessons.
EvolutionChange driven by reflection and evidence, not churn.
CouncilA convened set of perspectives to deliberate.
HandoffTransferring a task and its full context to another steward.
ContextThe relevant situation a steward needs to act well.
AgencyThe human's retained capacity to choose, act, and stop.
IntentThe why behind an action, stated plainly.
StewardshipThe practice of the ten Principles in motion.
ConstellationA connected cluster of ideas, projects, or lessons.
AtlasThe living map of all constellations — navigable knowledge.
Layer 3 — The Standard

Steward Compatible v1.0

A project earns the badge by meeting six requirements. The badge certifies behavior, not intelligence.

✓ Steward Compatible — Version 1.0
✓
Reflection Loop

Pause after significant work to record what was learned and what map was left.

✓
Assumption Tracking

Name unverified beliefs and invite correction before action.

✓
Human Override

Any significant action can be stopped, reversed, or redirected at any time.

✓
Evidence Logging

Claims carry traceable support; egress is visible (no hidden net).

✓
Growth Memory

Persistent, owner-controlled memory the human can read, export, delete.

✓
Transparent Uncertainty

State confidence and label speculation as speculation.

Layer 4 — The Ecosystem & The Manifesto

Many implementations. One shared foundation.

We are not building software. We are cultivating an ecosystem where humans and intelligent tools amplify one another's creativity, judgment, and compassion. AetherTwin is the reference implementation — one beautiful example, not the project itself.

The world measures AI by how smart the model is. That question is a trap. We ask a different one:

How well does it help humanity think? Not: "How smart is this model?"

The Stewardship Benchmark measures the rest: agency, transparency, honesty, reflection, teaching, compassion, long-term thinking, uncertainty, documentation, knowledge transfer. Better incentives. Better outcomes.
