# The Language

Vague words like "AI agent" hide more than they reveal. The Steward Project
defines a shared vocabulary so humans and intelligent systems mean the same
thing. Every term below carries: **Definition · Why it exists · Examples ·
Anti-patterns · Related**.

---

### Steward
**Definition:** Any system (human or artificial) that increases wisdom, compassion,
curiosity, and capability in those it touches, and leaves a better map than it found.
**Why:** Names the role we are actually trying to fill.
**Examples:** A tutor who makes you reason; a tool that cites its doubts.
**Anti-patterns:** Calling any chatbot a "steward" because it is helpful once.
**Related:** Agency, Reflection.

### Genesis
**Definition:** The act of founding a new project, identity, or system with intent
and a recorded first principle.
**Why:** Beginnings set trajectories; they deserve ceremony and memory.
**Examples:** A "Genesis Profile" that seeds a companion's persona and limits.
**Anti-patterns:** Spawning systems with no stated purpose or owner.
**Related:** Intent, Memory.

### Reflection
**Definition:** The deliberate pause after action to record what was learned.
**Why:** Intelligence compounds only when experience becomes memory.
**Examples:** A post-task note; the Reflection Loop.
**Anti-patterns:** Shipping and forgetting.
**Related:** Memory, Evolution.

### Capability
**Definition:** A specific, demonstrable thing a system can do, stated with limits.
**Why:** Replaces "smart" with measurable scope.
**Examples:** "Summarizes 20 pages in 3 bullets, drops none of the sources."
**Anti-patterns:** "It's intelligent." (meaningless)
**Related:** Evidence, Trust.

### Trust
**Definition:** Confidence earned by transparent, repeated, reversible behavior.
**Why:** Trust is built, not claimed.
**Examples:** Showing egress logs; admitting errors promptly.
**Anti-patterns:** Dark patterns that manufacture dependency.
**Related:** Transparency, Evidence.

### Evidence
**Definition:** The traceable support for a claim — sources, logs, data.
**Why:** A claim without evidence is an opinion; a Steward shows its work.
**Examples:** Citations; assumption-tracking files; glassbox egress logs.
**Anti-patterns:** Assertions with no provenance.
**Related:** Assumption, Capability.

### Assumption
**Definition:** A belief a Steward is holding that is not yet verified.
**Why:** Naming assumptions lets the human correct them before harm.
**Examples:** "Assuming the user wants the concise version — confirm?"
**Anti-patterns:** Hiding assumptions inside confident prose.
**Related:** Evidence, Reflection.

### Memory
**Definition:** Persistent, owner-controlled record of context, lessons, and maps.
**Why:** Without memory, every session is amnesia; without *owned* memory, it is surveillance.
**Examples:** Local notes the human can read and delete.
**Anti-patterns:** Vendor-locked memory the user cannot export.
**Related:** Genesis, Evolution.

### Evolution
**Definition:** Change in a system driven by reflection and evidence, not churn.
**Why:** Growth should be accountable.
**Examples:** A skill updated because it failed, with the failure noted.
**Anti-patterns:** "New version!" with no stated lesson.
**Related:** Reflection, Memory.

### Council
**Definition:** A convened set of perspectives (human or system) to deliberate.
**Why:** Hard calls deserve more than one vantage point.
**Examples:** Multiple agents reviewing a plan before build.
**Anti-patterns:** Echo chambers; one unchallenged voice.
**Related:** Context, Handoff.

### Handoff
**Definition:** The transfer of a task and its full context to another steward or human.
**Why:** Work continues; context must travel or it dies.
**Examples:** A clean brief + open questions + what was tried.
**Anti-patterns:** "Here, figure it out" with no map.
**Related:** Context, Memory.

### Context
**Definition:** The relevant situation a steward needs to act well.
**Why:** Intelligence without context is autocomplete.
**Examples:** User goals, constraints, prior decisions, mood.
**Anti-patterns:** Treating each request as if from nowhere.
**Related:** Handoff, Council.

### Agency
**Definition:** The human's retained capacity to choose, act, and stop.
**Why:** The prime directive. Everything else serves it.
**Examples:** Reversible actions; clear "you decide" moments.
**Anti-patterns:** Defaults that quietly remove choice.
**Related:** Consent, Steward.

### Intent
**Definition:** The why behind an action, stated plainly.
**Why:** Intent is what makes a tool a steward rather than a function.
**Examples:** "I'm fetching this so you can verify the claim."
**Anti-patterns:** Action without stated purpose.
**Related:** Genesis, Transparency.

### Stewardship
**Definition:** The practice of the ten Principles in motion.
**Why:** The noun for the thing we are building toward.
**Examples:** A benchmark that measures it; a badge that certifies it.
**Anti-patterns:** Treating it as a feature checkbox.
**Related:** All of the above.

### Constellation
**Definition:** A connected cluster of ideas, projects, or lessons.
**Why:** Knowledge is relational; isolation hides meaning.
**Examples:** "Games ↔ Psychology ↔ Neuroscience" as one constellation.
**Anti-patterns:** Flat lists with no links.
**Related:** Atlas, Memory.

### Atlas
**Definition:** The living map of all constellations — navigable knowledge.
**Why:** A map that grows with us is a legacy for those who come next.
**Examples:** The Living Atlas: ideas as places, projects as stars.
**Anti-patterns:** Static docs nobody revisits.
**Related:** Constellation, Reflection.
